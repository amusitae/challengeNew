Palestine Governorates

Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a historic city famous for religious sites and cultural heritage.

Hebron

Hebron is an ancient city known for its old markets and historic architecture.

Jenin

Jenin is in the north of the West Bank with a strong agricultural tradition.

Jericho

One of the oldest inhabited cities in the world, located near the Jordan Valley.

Nablus

Nablus is famous for historic soap-making and old city markets.

Qalqilya

Qalqilya is a small city near the coastal plains, with a tight-knit community.

Quds (Jerusalem)

Al-Quds (Jerusalem) is a city of profound historical and religious importance.

Ramallah

Ramallah is a modern administrative and cultural center in the West Bank.

Deir al-Balah

Deir al-Balah is located in the central Gaza Strip and is known for date palms.

Gaza

Gaza is the largest city in the Gaza Strip with a long coastal history.

Khan Yunis

Khan Yunis is a major city in the south of the Gaza Strip.

Rafah

Rafah sits at the southern tip of the Gaza Strip, bordering Egypt.

Tulkarm

Tulkarm is a West Bank agricultural and commercial center.

Tubas

Tubas is in the northeastern West Bank, near the Jordan Valley.

Salfit

Salfit is a smaller governorate known for olive groves and hills.
